project overview

PSA: This page is still being updated.
Our project client was the R-Pad Robotics Research Lab at Carnegie Mellon University. The goal of the project was to help the lab build a usable, intuitive virtual reality (VR) environment for users to complete a range of tasks, which a robotic arm can then learn from or mimic. We aimed to facilitate the gathering of different task data through the use of virtual reality headsets and spatial computing. This data and method of robot teaching can allow for remote teleoperation for tasks that cannot be done in person. There are two major applications to prototype for: 1) the VR environment with its spatial interfaces and control mechanisms and 2) a two-dimensional screen interface for the researcher moderating the task collection from the VR user. We also created a final proof of concept video to showcase the VR interface in its ideal future state.


primary questions

  • What is the primary user perspective while operating the robot?
  • Is a moderator necessary for the collection of task data?
  • How would the user interact with the interface and the environment?
  • What would the future of virtual reality teleoperation be like?
